An Annotated Guide to Your Loan Estimate

This guide is based on the official form from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).

Pro Tip: Make Lenders Compete for Your Business

1. Shop Around: Get at least 3-4 Loan Estimates from different types of lenders (e.g., your personal bank, a credit union, and a mortgage broker). This is the single best way to save money.

2. Ask Them to Beat Each Other: Once you have multiple offers, don't be afraid to play them against each other. Go to your preferred lender with a better offer from a competitor and ask, "Can you beat this deal?"

3. Lock Your Rate: Once you have an offer you are happy with, ask the lender to "lock" the interest rate. A rate lock prevents your interest rate from changing before you close.

Pro Tip: Demystifying "No-Cost" Refinancing

Be wary of offers for a "no-cost" or "no-out-of-pocket-cost" refinance. These loans are not truly free. The closing costs are typically handled in one of two ways:

  • Rolled into the loan: The costs are added to your new loan balance, meaning you are financing them and paying interest on them for the life of the loan.
  • Paid via a higher rate: The lender gives you a credit to cover the costs, but charges you a higher interest rate.

This isn't always a bad deal, but it's a trade-off. Use the calculator to see if it makes sense for your situation.

Pro-Tip: How to Truly Compare Lenders

To get a true apples-to-apples comparison, ask each lender for a quote at the exact same interest rate. With the rate held constant, you can then directly compare the fees in Section A: Origination Charges.

Also, check for any Lender Credits. This is money the lender gives you to offset closing costs and should be clearly listed in Section J on Page 2. A large credit can make a loan with high fees much more attractive.

Pro Tip: Ask About the 'Reissue Rate' on Title Insurance

When refinancing, you are typically required to purchase a new lender's title insurance policy. However, if your existing mortgage is only a few years old, you may be eligible for a significant discount on this new policy. This is often called a "reissue rate," "refinance rate," or "reissue discount."

Action Required: The availability and terms of this discount vary significantly by state and are not always offered automatically. You should always ask your lender or settlement agent directly: "Do I qualify for a reissue rate on my title insurance?"
